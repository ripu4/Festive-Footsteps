Onam

Onam: A Time for Family, Food, and Festivities!

Onam is a vibrant and joyous festival celebrated in Kerala, India, marking the annual harvest and welcoming the mythical King Mahabali. This festival usually falls in the month of Chingam (August-September) and lasts for ten days, culminating in the grand celebration of Thiruvonam..


Preparations

Homes and public spaces are cleaned thoroughly and decorated with flowers, especially pookalam (flower rangoli), made of colorful blooms arranged in intricate patterns.

Families purchase new clothes and essentials to celebrate the festival. Traditional attire, such as the mundum neriyathum for men and kasavu sarees for women, is popular.


Celebrating Onam

Every morning, families create or refresh their pookalam with seasonal flowers, often incorporating designs that symbolize prosperity and joy.

Prepare a variety of traditional dishes as offerings to the deities. This typically includes sadya, a banquet of vegetarian dishes served on a banana leaf.

Start the day with a ceremonial bath and wearing new clothes and Families perform rituals to welcome the spirit of King Mahabali. This may include placing a new plantain leaf at the entrance of the home as a symbol of hospitality.

On the day of Thiruvonam, a grand feast known as Onam Sadya is served. It includes a variety of dishes like rice, sambar, avial, thoran, payasam, and other delicacies, typically served on a banana leaf.

Everyone sits together to enjoy the meal, symbolizing unity and togetherness.

Various cultural events and competitions are organized, such as puli kali (tiger dance), kummattikali (mask dance), and Onam boat races. Local communities often participate in these events, showcasing their talents.

Traditional games like vadam vali (tug of war), kilithattukal (egg tapping), and musical chairs are played, adding to the festive spirit.

It’s customary to visit friends and relatives during Onam, exchanging greetings and gifts to strengthen social bonds.



Best Ways to Celebrate Onam

Engage in community events, such as boat races and cultural performances. This not only enhances the festive spirit but also brings people together.

Involve family members in making a pookalam at your entrance, using fresh flowers to welcome guests and the spirit of Mahabali.

Prepare a traditional Onam Sadya for family and friends. Encourage everyone to wear traditional attire and enjoy the meal together on banana leaves.

Organize or attend workshops on making traditional Onam dishes or learning folk dances associated with the festival. This promotes cultural awareness and preserves traditions.

Use traditional decorations like banana plants, coconut leaves, and lights to create a festive ambiance at home. Displaying traditional art forms and crafts adds to the celebration.
