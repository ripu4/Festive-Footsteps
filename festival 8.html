Bihu

Bihu: Where Every Dance Tells a Story of Prosperity!

Bihu is the most significant festival of Assam, India, celebrated by the Assamese people. It marks the Assamese New Year and the arrival of spring, symbolizing the beginning of the agricultural season. Bihu is celebrated three times a year: Rongali Bihu (Bohag Bihu), Kongali Bihu (Kati Bihu), and Bhogali Bihu (Magh Bihu). The most celebrated is Rongali Bihu, which typically falls in mid-April.


Preparations

Before the festival, families clean their homes and surroundings to welcome the New Year. Homes are often decorated with mokholot, traditional bamboo structures adorned with flowers and leaves.

FFamilies prepare a variety of traditional dishes, including pitha (rice cakes), larus (sweet balls), and other Assamese delicacies. Special preparations are made for feasting, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.


Celebrating Bihu

Bihu is marked by various cultural activities, including traditional dance and music. People dress in traditional Assamese attire, like mekhela chador for women and dhoti for men. Dance forms like Bihu Naas are performed, showcasing joy and celebration.

The Bihu dance is an integral part of the celebrations, with people dancing in groups to the rhythm of traditional instruments like dhol (drum), pepah (flute), and gogona (a traditional instrument).

Communities gather to celebrate together. Public events often include singing, dancing, and sharing food, fostering a sense of unity and joy.

On the day of Rongali Bihu, families may perform rituals to honor deities, thanking them for the harvest and seeking blessings for a prosperous year ahead.

It is customary to visit relatives, friends, and neighbors during Bihu, exchanging greetings and sharing festive food, which strengthens community bonds.

During Bhogali Bihu, community bonfires called Makar Sakar are lit, and people gather around to celebrate with music, dance, and feasting on traditional foods like pitha.

Best Ways to Celebrate Bihu

Participate in local Bihu celebrations and fairs to experience the vibrant culture, including music, dance, and traditional games.

Engage family members in cooking traditional Assamese dishes, especially pitha and other delicacies. Hosting a Bihu feast can enhance the festive spirit.

Embrace the essence of Bihu by wearing traditional Assamese clothing. This adds to the authenticity of the celebration and honors the cultural heritage.

Take part in or organize Bihu dance workshops to learn the traditional moves and rhythms, fostering a deeper connection to the culture.

Create a festive atmosphere at home with traditional decorations using bamboo, flowers, and lights. This sets the mood for the celebrations.
